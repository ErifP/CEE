Relaxation Tips

Winding down is an important part of getting ready for sleep. See our Routine Page for more information.

There are many ways to do this, but here are a few ideas and tips to get you started:

  • Try to avoid using electronic devices such as smartphones and tablets for about an hour before you go to bed as the light from these devices may have a negative effect on sleep
  • Using one of the many apps available to aid sleep (see our technology page)
  • A warm bath or shower (not too hot) can help get your body to an ideal temperature for resting
  • Writing down any concerns or tasks for the following day can help organise your thoughts and clear your mind of any distractions
  • If you find your mind is focussing on distracting thoughts you can try doing a mental puzzle, repeating words (such as counting sheep), or focussing on other images or thoughts. These are part of a technique called thought blocking and can be part of a more structured programme to help with insomnia, further information here
  • Light stretching or relaxation exercise, such as some non-strenuous yoga, can help to relax the muscles. Do not do anything too vigorous however, as this can have a negative effect (see our Exercise Page)
  • Meditation and breathing exercises can be very useful. Audio guidance can be found on apps, CDs and online if you don’t have any existing techniques
  • Reading or listening to an audiobook or the radio can relax the mind through distraction
  • Avoid caffeine and tobacco in the evenings – these are both stimulants and can keep you awake
  • Do not over indulge in food and alcohol before bed (see our Diet Page)
  • Try using your bed just for sleeping and sexual activity – this makes you associate going to bed with sleeping and relaxing
  • Create a positive sleeping environment:
  • Keep your bed clean, tidy and comfortable
  • Ensure you are not too hot or cold in bed – make sure you have adequate blankets and your heating is not set too high for your comfort
  • Ensure there are no lights that distract or wake you – think about whether your curtains are blocking enough light in the morning if the sunrise disturbs your sleep
  • Try to prevent any noises or night time disturbances (where possible) – ear plugs can be helpful when noises are unavoidable (for example traffic or neighbours)
