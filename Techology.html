Technology - Good or Bad?

The use of technology can impact your sleep in both positive and negative ways.

The Negatives

Supressing Melatonin

The screens of moblie phones, tablets, computers and TVs emit blue light which reduces the production of melatonin. This hormone helps to control your sleep/wake cycle so reducing the levels of melatonin in the body can make it harder to fall asleep and stay asleep.

Make sure you allow at least thrity minutes without a screen before going to sleep.

We suggest making your bedroom a gadget-free zone to remove the temptation!

Keeping Your Brain Alert

Surfing the internet or watching TV before bed can trick your brain into thinking it needs to stay awake. Maybe watching an exciting movie or looking at photos on social media can make it harder to relax and settle down to sleep.

Your brain needs a rest - particularly if you've been looking at a screen all day!

They Wake You Up.

Keeping a mobile phone or tablet by your bed can wake you up in the night thanks to late-night texts, emails, calls, or calendar reminders.

Almost two-thirds of children aged between six to 17 sleep with at least one electronic device in their bedroom, which leads to them getting less sleep on school nights compared with other kids, according to their parents. This can add up to almost an hour per night, and the quality of this sleep is negatively affected too.

To get a better night’s slumber, parents can limit their kids’ technology use in the bedroom, and parent should set a good example by doing the same!

The Positives

There are many apps and products on the market which can be exceedingly helpful to get you into good sleep routines, help you monitor your nights and leave you feeling rested in the morning.

The Apps

Available on the play store or noisli.com

Create your own sleep soundtrack. This beautiful app lets you choose from a rnage of different sounds (like thunder, wind, white noise, and even the buzz of a coffee shop) to create your ideal sleep soundtrack.

Pzizz

Available through Play and Apple stores and pzizz.com

With Pzizz, you can set a timer for the length of sleep session so it plays a soothing dreamscape (a combination of music, voiceovers, and sound effects) while you sleep; then, you can wake up to the built-in alarm. You'll need to upgrade for more advanced features, like the ability to choose different sounds, but the app offers a seven-day free trial.

Slumber

slumber.fm or though the Apple store.

Slumber offers a combination of experiences to help you fall asleep, whether it's meditation focused, a six-part bedtime story series, or realxing white noise. New sleep-inducing stories and meditations are added each week, and you can get many episodes free without upgrading to the premium version.

Sleep Cycle App

sleepcycle.com or through Play and Apple stores.

Using the microphone and accelerometer on your phone, this app tracks your sleep patterns and wakes you up gently during light sleep so you'll wake up feeling refreshed.
